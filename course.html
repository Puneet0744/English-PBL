Communicative English

Course Overview (2024–2025)

The Communicative English course (Course Code: 21LEH101T) aims to develop essential language skills in reading, writing, speaking, and digital literacy. It equips students with the tools needed for academic success, career readiness, and effective participation in a global environment.

Key Modules

  • Academic Reading Skills: Skimming, scanning, summarizing, and deep comprehension.
  • Technical Writing Skills: Paragraphs, essays, reports, and creative writing.
  • English in the Digital World: Netiquette, online tools, and responsible communication.
  • English for Employability: Interviews, group discussions, resumes, and job skills.
  • Business English: Professional presentations, reports, and vocabulary.

Literature & Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)

Students explore global issues through the following novels:

  • The Palace of Illusions – Gender and power.
  • The Hungry Tide – Environmental relationships.
  • 1984 – Freedom and surveillance.
  • To Kill a Mockingbird – Justice and inequality.
  • Brave New World – Technology vs individuality.
Outcome: Linking literature to global challenges for critical thinking and awareness.

Learning Methods

  • Debates, group discussions, and presentations
  • Role plays, seminars, and case studies
  • Digital research, peer reviews, and projects

Assessment

  • 60% Internal: Tests, assignments, and activities
  • 40% Final Exam

Learning Outcomes

  • Read and analyze complex texts
  • Communicate effectively in various contexts
  • Utilize digital platforms responsibly
  • Demonstrate employability and business skills
